2. Scope of this policy

This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information when you:

  • Visit or use the Future Signals web app, browser extension, or related services (collectively, the "Service").
  • Interact with us via email, support tickets, social media, or events.

It does not cover third-party websites or services you may access through links in our product.

3. Information we collect

3.1 Information you provide directly

  • Account & profile data: Name, email, organization, role, password (hashed), profile photo, bio, preferences.
  • Content you submit: Inputs (links, files, summaries, tags, notes), Cluster definitions, and any other information you add or upload.
  • Communications: Support requests, survey responses, feedback forms.

3.2 Information collected automatically

  • Usage data: Pages viewed, features used, clicks, timestamps, referring/exit pages, search queries.
  • Device & log data: IP address, browser type/version, operating system, device identifiers.
  • Cookies & similar technologies: See Section 9 (Cookies & Tracking Technologies).

3.3 Information from third parties

  • Analytics/marketing partners: Aggregate data about how users found or interact with the Service.
  • Public or commercial sources: Public websites or databases we use to enrich Input metadata (e.g., to auto-fetch titles, images, or summaries of URLs you add).

3.4 AI/LLM processing

To power features like auto-tagging, summarization, clustering, or recommendation, we may send snippets of user-provided content (e.g., Input titles/descriptions) to a third-party AI provider — currently OpenAI. We minimize the data sent and apply contractual controls. See Section 6 for the list of processors we work with.

3.5 Browser extension

The Future Signals browser extension only reads the content of a webpage when you actively use it — opening the extension's side panel or capturing an Input from the page you're viewing. At that moment, it reads the page's title, URL, any text you've selected, and its meta description, and uses this to pre-fill your capture. The extension does not run in the background, does not access pages you haven't opened it on, and does not collect any browsing data outside of that moment of active use.

4. Why we use your information

We process personal information to:

  1. Provide and maintain the Service (create accounts, authenticate users, display and organize Inputs).
  2. Improve and personalize the Service (feature development, UX research, A/B testing, performance monitoring).
  3. Communicate with you (service notices, feature updates, marketing — with your consent where required).
  4. Ensure security & prevent abuse (detecting spam, fraud, unauthorized access).
  5. Comply with legal obligations (tax, accounting, regulatory requests, enforcing terms of service).

5. Legal bases (EEA/UK/Swiss users)

If you are located in the EEA/UK/Switzerland, we rely on these legal bases:

  • Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): To provide the Service you signed up for.
